rental price analysis

Visualization and analysis of rental price on individual level, on neighborhood level and within/outside the MBTA buffer

Price of Rental Unit Analysis

We plotted individual rental units on map and categorized price with different colors (lighter color means lower price and darker color means higher price). You may zoom in to see how units of different prices distribute in one neighborhood.

Price of Neighborhood Analysis

We aggregated prices on neighborhood level and you may find the neighborhood that has highest/lowest average renting price. We acknowledge that the aggregated prices do not distinguish unit type, so the fact that larger houses has higher price may make the average price less effective showing price per person/per bedroom.

Rental price within/outside MBTA buffer

We aggregated prices near MBTA stations within 500-meter-radius buffer and showed the average price on neighborhood level. But not all the neighborhood have post. You may find the highest rental price near Porter Square T station and the lowest price near Alewife T station.

Housing count analysis

Visualization and analysis of housing count on individual level and on neighborhood level

Housing clusters

Here is the map showing how available houses/units cluster. You may expect to find lots of posts if the circle is bigger, together with a large number indicating the exact number of posts in the area.

Neighborhood count

This map shows the count of posts in each neighborhood in Cambridge and Somerville. We can tell that East Cambridge, WInter Hill, Davis Square, Union Square are some popular neighborhoods that have most posts.

room type analysis

Visualized the spatial distribution of houses/units and summarized the count of unit type by the number of bedrooms

Room Type Analysis

This map shows the spatial distribution of houses/units, differentiated by the number of bedrooms. Our idea is to differentiate rooms that can accommodate different number of people. In this case, we combine studio and one bedroom type to 1B. We can tell that the most popular room type is 1B, and the more the bedrooms are, the less available units are.

Type Summary

We summarized the count of unit type by the number of bedrooms. What offered most is 1 Bedroom, where it could be studio, 1B1B, 1B2B, etc. The more bedrooms in the apartment are available, the less offerings exist. But there are still offerings for 7 bedrooms and 8 bedrooms—if you have a big family or wish to live with many friends, you do not want to miss those!

word cloud

Extracted keywords from the description section, displayed the most frequent words with which landlords advertise their housing

Word Cloud

We parsed descriptions in the Craigslist posts and found effective words that show up over 100 times in the description. The most frequent word is “month”. Location keywords like Cambridge, Davis, Harvard, Central are the popular ones. Amenities like how water, parking, kitchen, heat, laundry, etc. are mentioned many times.

Bar chart

Here is an overview of some selected key words. Of 4,000 appropriate posts, “month” is mentioned most times (over 6,000 times). “Pet” is also a hot word, which is mentioned over 1,500 times. Among amenities, “hot water”, “parking”, “laundry”, “kitchen” are mostly used to advertise the property. Among adjectives, modern, spacious, amazing, furnished are popular ones. There are other interesting features that the landlords think can boost their property value like (close to) redline, (close to) school, gym, etc.

history analysis

Besides parsing Boston Craigslist in April, we also used two-year Craiglist data provided by Boston Metropolitan Area Planning Council and prof. Joseph Ferreira at DUSP at MIT.

Posting time

This map displays the temporal distribution of rental house in Cambridge and Somerville in one year. Craigslist postings from March, 2015 to March 2016 were collected and visualized. The result shows that most rental houses are posted during October - December, 2015 and February- March, 2016

Average Price

When we divide posts according to their posting time into four seasons of the year, we can see the average renting price in Cambridge fluctuates between 1700 and 1900 per bedroom and a slight increase of renting price per bedroom in Somerville from $800 to $1200+. In general, renting price in Cambridge is much higher than that in Somerville.
